Nitrogen AI provides four assessment modules for teams that need structured analytical documents alongside financial deliverables. Unlike the document-generation modules, assessments use a multi-layer workspace flow: you review and refine each layer of the analysis before the AI drafts the final document. All outputs include inline citations drawn from your uploaded evidence.

## How assessment modules work

Every assessment module follows the same three-stage pattern:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Setup">
    Configure the scope of the assessment — geography, sector, and other framing parameters. The AI pre-fills defaults from your project context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build layers">
    Work through two or three structured layers. Each layer builds on the previous one (e.g., outline → details, or risk themes → specific risks → mitigations). Review and edit the AI-generated content before moving to the next layer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate output">
    Once all layers are confirmed, click **Generate Output**. The module drafts the full document with per-section narrative, inline citations, and an executive summary.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Assessment modules open an **assessment workspace** panel in your project chat — a structured multi-layer form that stays open while you work through the analysis.
</Note>

## Stakeholder Assessment

The Stakeholder Assessment module maps and profiles the key stakeholders relevant to your project — who they are, what interests they hold, and how to engage them.

**Module ID**: `stakeholder_assessment`  
**Output type**: Assessment document (DOCX)  
**When to use it**: Any project that involves community engagement, government approvals, partnerships, or donor reporting.

### Workflow layers

| Layer | Type | What you review |
|---|---|---|
| Outline | Simple list | Stakeholder categories / groupings |
| Details | Structured list | Named stakeholders per category with roles and interests |

### Setup fields

- **Geography / Location** — where the project is located (e.g., "Northern Ghana")
- **Sector** — Energy Access, Clean Cooking, Water & Sanitation, Agriculture, Health, Education, Financial Inclusion, or Other

## Landscape Mapping

The Landscape Mapping module documents the ecosystem of actors, initiatives, and dynamics surrounding your project — useful for positioning your project within the broader sector context.

**Module ID**: `landscape_mapping`  
**Output type**: Assessment document (DOCX)  
**When to use it**: Funding applications, strategy development, competitive analysis, and sector gap identification.

### Workflow layers

| Layer | Type | What you review |
|---|---|---|
| Outline | Simple list | High-level themes or categories in the landscape |
| Details | Structured list | Specific entities, actors, or initiatives per theme |

### Setup fields

- **Region** — geographic focus of the mapping (e.g., "Sub-Saharan Africa", "Kenya")
- **Sector** — the sector to map

## Environmental & Social Management Plan (ESMP)

The ESMP module drafts an IFC-aligned Environmental and Social Management Plan covering risks, mitigation measures, and a monitoring schedule. It follows international safeguard standards used by DFIs, the World Bank, and climate funds.

**Module ID**: `esmp`  
**Output type**: Assessment document (DOCX)  
**When to use it**: Projects requiring DFI financing, grant compliance with IFC Performance Standards, ESIA processes, or climate finance applications.

### Workflow layers

| Layer | Type | What you review |
|---|---|---|
| Risk themes | Simple list | High-level E&S risk themes (e.g., land acquisition, community health) |
| Risks | Structured list | Specific risks per theme with severity and likelihood |
| Mitigation | Structured list | Mitigation measures and monitoring indicators per risk |

<Tip>
  The ESMP module is keyed to terminology used in IFC Performance Standards and CDM/Gold Standard safeguard requirements. It works best when your evidence includes existing E&S screening reports or feasibility studies.
</Tip>

## Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL) Plan

The MEL Plan module builds a results framework with indicators, data sources, and a data collection plan — suitable for grant reporting, impact investor due diligence, and internal M&E.

**Module ID**: `mel_plan`  
**Output type**: Assessment document (DOCX)  
**When to use it**: Grant applications, donor reporting frameworks, IRIS+ alignment, SDG reporting, and theory-of-change documentation.

### Workflow layers

| Layer | Type | What you review |
|---|---|---|
| Dimensions | Simple list | Result chain levels or thematic impact dimensions (e.g., Output, Outcome, Impact) |
| Indicators | Structured list | Specific indicators per dimension with targets and baselines |
| Data plan | Structured list | Data collection method, frequency, responsible party, and source per indicator |

<Note>
  MEL Plan outputs are structured around the result chain levels you define — they are not locked to a specific framework. You can align to IRIS+, logframe, or any results framework that matches your funder requirements.
</Note>
