The Investment Memo module produces a publication-ready Word document that gives funders, investment committees, and internal reviewers a clear, evidence-backed recommendation on your project. You provide the context; Nitrogen AI reads your uploaded evidence, queries the platform's case study corpus, and writes every section — including inline citations that trace each claim back to a source.

## What you get

The output is a `.docx` file containing a fully written memo, structured around the sections you approve in the alignment step. A typical memo includes:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Executive Summary" icon="file-lines">
    A 2–3 paragraph overview of the project, the investment ask, and the key recommendation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recommendation" icon="circle-check">
    A clear decision — proceed, hold, or reject — with a stated confidence level and any conditions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Rationale" icon="list">
    Detailed justification covering strategic alignment, team track record, market opportunity, and path to impact.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Evidence Summary" icon="book-open">
    Key findings from your uploaded materials and relevant case study insights from the corpus.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Risks & Assumptions" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Technical, financial, and operational risks alongside the key assumptions underlying the analysis.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Open Questions" icon="circle-question">
    Outstanding due diligence items and clarifications needed from the applicant.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
The section list above is the default. During the alignment step you can add, remove, rename, or reorder any section before generation begins.
</Note>

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Provide project context">
    Nitrogen AI pre-fills what it can from your project workspace — title, geography, target beneficiaries, and goals. You can review and adjust these details before moving forward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the alignment">
    Before generating a single word of prose, Nitrogen AI produces a proposed memo outline. Each section shows its title, a short description, and 3–5 specific key points it will cover for *your* project. You can accept the outline as-is, give feedback in plain language ("make the Risks section more conservative", "add a section on team capacity"), or toggle sections on and off.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and generate">
    Once you confirm the alignment, Nitrogen AI writes the full memo. Citations are inserted inline using numbered references that map to a bibliography at the end of the document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export to DOCX">
    Download the finished memo as a `.docx` file, ready for editing in Microsoft Word or Google Docs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inputs

All inputs are optional — Nitrogen AI infers sensible defaults from your project workspace. Providing more detail produces a more specific, higher-quality memo.

<ParamField path="body.project_title" type="string">
  The name of the project as it should appear in the memo heading. Defaults to your workspace title.

  *Example: Solar Mini-Grid Pilot in Northern Kenya*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.geography" type="string">
  Country, region, or site where the project will take place.

  *Example: Kenya, Northern Region*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.target_beneficiaries" type="string">
  Who will directly benefit from the project.

  *Example: Rural households without grid access, approximately 12,000 people*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.project_goal" type="string">
  The main objective or intended outcome of the project.

  *Example: Provide reliable electricity access to off-grid communities through a solar mini-grid*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.budget_range" type="string">
  The expected funding ask or total project cost.

  *Example: $500,000 – $750,000*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.timeline" type="string">
  Project duration or key milestones timeline.

  *Example: 18 months; construction Q3 2025, operations from Q1 2026*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.key_risks" type="string">
  Any known risks, constraints, or sensitivities you want the memo to address.

  *Example: Regulatory approval for grid interconnection is pending*
</ParamField>

## Alignment options

During the review step, two additional parameters control the writing style:

<ParamField path="body.tone" type="string" default="balanced">
  Writing register for the memo. Options: `conservative` (emphasises risks and uncertainties), `balanced` (objective presentation of pros and cons), `optimistic` (highlights opportunities while noting risks).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.detail_level" type="string" default="standard">
  How much depth each section goes into. Options: `concise`, `standard`, `comprehensive`.
</ParamField>

## Citations and evidence

The memo only cites sources that exist in your project — either documents you have uploaded to the workspace or items from the Nitrogen AI platform corpus. The AI will never invent a citation. If it makes a claim based on general reasoning rather than a specific source, it states that explicitly.

<Tip>
Upload relevant reports, feasibility studies, or previous project documents to your workspace before running this module. The more evidence you provide, the better-grounded the memo will be.
</Tip>
