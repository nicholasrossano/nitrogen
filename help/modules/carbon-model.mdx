The Carbon Emissions Calculator estimates how many tonnes of CO₂ equivalent your project will avoid or reduce each year compared to the baseline scenario. It supports seven project types — from improved cookstoves to grid-connected renewables — and aligns its calculation approach with recognised carbon standards including Gold Standard and CDM methodologies. Nitrogen AI extracts inputs from your conversation and documents, fills gaps with methodology-appropriate defaults, and shows you exactly what it assumed.

## Supported project types

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Improved cookstoves" icon="fire">
    Biomass-to-biomass efficiency improvements. Uses fraction of non-renewable biomass (fNRB) and fuel consumption data.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fuel switch" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Switching from biomass to LPG, biogas, or ethanol. Accounts for project-side fuel emissions and leakage.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Safe water" icon="droplet">
    Water purification replacing fuel-based boiling. Based on population served and water consumption per person.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grid renewable energy" icon="bolt">
    Grid-connected solar, wind, hydro, or geothermal. Uses installed capacity, capacity factor, and grid emission factor.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Solar home systems" icon="sun">
    Off-grid solar displacing kerosene lamps and diesel generators. Uses system count, capacity, and peak sun hours.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Biodigesters" icon="seedling">
    Animal manure biodigesters capturing methane and replacing cooking fuel. Based on livestock type and herd size.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Efficient lighting" icon="lightbulb">
    LED or CFL lamps replacing incandescent bulbs. Uses lamp count, wattage delta, and grid emission factor.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What it calculates

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Net emission reductions" icon="arrow-trend-down">
    The headline output: baseline emissions minus project emissions minus leakage, in tCO₂e per year.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Baseline emissions" icon="chart-bar">
    What emissions would have occurred in the absence of the project, based on fuel consumption and emission factors.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Project emissions" icon="chart-simple">
    Residual emissions from the project activity itself (e.g. combustion of project-side fuel).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Leakage" icon="arrow-right">
    Emissions that shift elsewhere as a result of the project (e.g. displaced demand moving to an alternative source).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Crediting period totals" icon="calendar">
    Cumulative net emission reductions across the full crediting period (typically 7–10 years).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sensitivity analysis" icon="chart-line">
    How net ERs change as fNRB, adoption rate, or fuel consumption vary from their base values.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Nitrogen AI identifies the project type">
    From your conversation and documents, the model identifies which methodology pack to use — cookstoves, fuel switch, grid renewable, and so on. You can also specify the project type directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inputs are extracted and filled">
    The model reads your uploaded materials for numeric values and fills any gaps with methodology-standard defaults. Each assumed value is clearly marked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and edit the inputs table">
    An inputs table appears in the chat. Edit any value and the net ERs recalculate immediately. You can switch to a different project type at any time — user-confirmed values are preserved where they apply to the new methodology.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export the model">
    Download a formula-driven Excel workbook with a full emission reduction schedule, inputs provenance, and sensitivity tables.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inputs

The relevant inputs depend on your project type. Commonly used fields are listed below.

### Shared across most project types

<ParamField path="body.method_pack" type="string" required>
  The project type, which determines the calculation methodology.

  Options: `cookstoves`, `fuel_switch`, `safe_water`, `grid_renewable`, `solar_home`, `biodigester`, `efficient_lighting`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.usage_rate" type="number" default="1.0">
  Fraction of project participants actively using the technology (0–1). A value of 1.0 means 100% usage.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.adoption_rate" type="number" default="1.0">
  Rate at which adoption ramps up over time (0–1). A value less than 1 applies a linear ramp-up across the crediting period.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.crediting_period_years" type="number" default="10">
  The number of years over which emission reductions are claimed, typically matching your carbon standard's crediting period.
</ParamField>

### Cookstoves and fuel switch

<ParamField path="body.devices_households" type="number" required>
  Number of devices or households the project reaches.

  *Example: 5000 cookstoves distributed to 5000 households*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.baseline_fuel_consumption_kg_yr" type="number" required>
  Average fuel consumed per household per year under the baseline scenario, in kg.

  *Example: 2000 kg/yr per household (traditional wood stove)*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.project_fuel_consumption_kg_yr" type="number">
  Average fuel consumed per household per year under the project scenario, in kg. If not provided, the model derives this from efficiency ratios or fuel savings percentage.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.fnrb" type="number" default="0.70">
  Fraction of non-renewable biomass (fNRB). The share of biomass sourced from non-sustainable harvesting. Ranges from 0 to 1; country-level defaults are used if not specified.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.baseline_fuel_type" type="string">
  The fuel displaced by the project.

  Options: `wood`, `charcoal`, `kerosene`, `dung`
</ParamField>

### Grid renewable energy

<ParamField path="body.installed_capacity_kw" type="number" required>
  Installed generation capacity in kW.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.capacity_factor" type="number" default="0.18 for solar; 0.30 for wind">
  Expected capacity factor as a decimal (0–1).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.grid_emission_factor" type="number">
  The emissions intensity of the grid being displaced, in tCO₂/MWh. Country-level defaults are applied if not provided.
</ParamField>

### Solar home systems

<ParamField path="body.num_systems" type="number" required>
  Number of solar home systems deployed.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.system_capacity_wp" type="number">
  Panel capacity per system in watts-peak (Wp).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.peak_sun_hours" type="number">
  Average peak sun hours per day at the project location.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.baseline_fuel_consumption_l_yr" type="number">
  Baseline kerosene or diesel consumption per household per year, in litres.
</ParamField>

### Biodigesters

<ParamField path="body.num_digesters" type="number" required>
  Number of biodigesters installed.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.livestock_type" type="string">
  Type of animals supplying manure to the digesters.

  Options: `dairy_cattle`, `other_cattle`, `swine`, `poultry`, `buffalo`, `sheep`, `goats`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.num_animals" type="number">
  Average number of animals per digester.
</ParamField>

### Efficient lighting

<ParamField path="body.num_lamps" type="number" required>
  Number of project lamps distributed.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.baseline_wattage" type="number" default="60">
  Wattage of the lamp being replaced, in watts.

  *Example: 60W incandescent*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.project_wattage" type="number" default="9">
  Wattage of the project lamp, in watts.

  *Example: 9W LED*
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="body.operating_hours_per_day" type="number" default="3.5">
  Daily lamp operating hours. Capped at 5.0 per methodology guidelines.
</ParamField>

## The exported Excel workbook

| Sheet | Contents |
|-------|----------|
| **Model** | Key inputs (editable), per-device emission factors, Year 1 results, and crediting period totals. Change any input and the whole workbook recalculates. |
| **ER Schedule** | Year-by-year baseline emissions, project emissions, leakage, and net ERs across the full crediting period. |
| **Inputs** | Full provenance table: every parameter with value, unit, methodology scope, source, and status. Assumed values highlighted in yellow. |
| **Sensitivity** | How net ERs shift as fNRB, adoption rate, fuel consumption, and other key parameters vary. |

<Tip>
The ER Schedule sheet is particularly useful for carbon finance applications. It shows year-by-year net ERs in the format most verification bodies expect.
</Tip>

<Warning>
Emission reduction estimates are indicative. They are based on the inputs you provide and the methodology defaults applied. Formal carbon credit claims require independent verification against the relevant standard (Gold Standard, CDM, Verra, etc.).
</Warning>
