---

This guide walks through the full Nitrogen AI workflow, from sign-up to your first exported assessment output.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up for Nitrogen AI">
    Go to [app.the-nitrogen.ai](https://app.the-nitrogen.ai) and create your account with your email address.

    New accounts start on the **trial tier**, which gives you limited AI generation credits to explore the platform.

    <Tip>
      If your organization already uses Nitrogen AI, ask an existing member to invite you to their workspace instead of signing up separately — see [Workspaces](/concepts/workspaces).
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Describe your project">
    Start a new chat and describe your project in plain language — location, technology, scale, target population, and what you're trying to achieve.

    ```text
    Example: "We're developing a 5 MW solar-plus-storage mini-grid in rural Nigeria to
    serve 3,000 households and small businesses currently without reliable grid access.
    We're seeking development finance and need a project plan and an LCOE model."
    ```

    Nitrogen AI uses this description to create a project record and infer geography, sector, and target population.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload evidence">
    Open the project's **Files** view and upload supporting materials — feasibility studies, technical reports, financial models, or prior assessments.

    <Note>
      You can generate a project plan and run assessments without any evidence uploaded — Nitrogen AI will flag gaps and ask for the missing inputs conversationally. Uploading evidence first generally produces more specific, better-grounded results.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate your project plan">
    Ask Nitrogen AI to build your project plan, or it will offer to generate one once your description has enough detail (at minimum, a description with identifiable geography and technology).

    The plan organizes your work into pillars, each containing concrete items — some are assessments you can run directly, others are general deliverables to track manually.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run an assessment">
    From the project plan (or by asking in chat), launch an assessment relevant to your project — for example, the LCOE Model for a solar project. Each assessment opens in a dedicated workspace with a setup → build → output flow.

    Confirm each stage as you go; some steps auto-populate from your evidence and let you edit before confirming.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export your result">
    Once the assessment reaches its final stage and you confirm it, click **Export** to download the result as DOCX or XLSX, ready to share with investors, donors, or your internal team.
  </Step>
</Steps>
