---
title: 'Manage project status categories and overrides'
description: 'Add, edit, and refresh project status categories, and override a system-computed status when you know better than the model.'
---

This guide covers editing the categories behind your project's status dashboard, refreshing an assessment, and overriding a result.

## Edit a category's definition

Each status category is driven by an editable definition — the text describing what "healthy" looks like for that category on your project.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the status panel">
    From the project overview, open the project status section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a category to edit">
    Click a category card to open its editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the definition">
    Rewrite the definition to reflect what matters for your specific project — for example, tightening "Funding & economics" to focus on a specific financing structure you're pursuing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and refresh">
    Save your change, then refresh the category so Nitrogen AI re-assesses it against the new definition.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add a custom category

If the five default categories don't cover something important to your project, add your own.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the category editor">
    From the status panel, choose to add a new category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a definition, or generate structured criteria">
    Describe what "healthy" means in plain language. You can also have Nitrogen AI auto-generate structured criteria from your definition text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the category">
    The new category appears alongside the defaults and is assessed the same way.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can also delete a category you don't need — this only removes it from the dashboard and doesn't affect any evidence or assessments.

## Refresh a status

Status doesn't update on every project change automatically. Refresh a single category, or refresh all categories at once, whenever you want an up-to-date read — typically after adding new evidence or completing an assessment.

## Override a status

When you have context the AI doesn't — or simply disagree with its read — you can override any category's status directly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the category">
    Click into the category you want to override.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your status">
    Select the status you believe is correct (green, yellow, or red).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an explanation">
    Provide a short explanation for the override. This is required so the audit trail stays meaningful.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Overrides always take precedence over the system-computed status until you clear them. Every override records who made it, when, and the prior system status — so you can always see what the AI thought versus what a human decided.
</Info>

## Review sources

Every assessed category shows a **Sources** link back to the evidence documents or assessment instances it drew on. Use this to spot-check the AI's reasoning before deciding whether to trust or override a result.
