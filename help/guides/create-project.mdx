Creating a project is how you tell Nitrogen AI what you're working on. There's no form to fill out — you describe your project in a chat message, and Nitrogen AI extracts the structured details it needs to recommend the right assessments and ground every output in your project's context.

## What you'll need before you start

You don't need any files ready before creating a project. That said, having these details in mind makes your opening description more useful:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Geography" icon="map-pin">
    The country or region where the project operates. Be as specific as you can — district or province level is more useful than a country name alone.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Target population" icon="users">
    Who the project serves: households, smallholder farmers, health clinics, schools, and so on. Include an approximate number of beneficiaries if you have one.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sector or technology" icon="solar-panel">
    The technology or intervention at the center of the project — mini-grid electrification, clean cookstoves, solar irrigation, or climate adaptation, for example.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Goal" icon="bullseye">
    What the project is trying to achieve.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Create a new project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new chat">
    From your dashboard, start a new chat. This creates a project record as soon as you send your first message.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe your project">
    Write a plain-language description of what the project is and what it's trying to achieve.

    ```text
    Example: "A 500 kW solar mini-grid in rural northern Ghana serving 2,000
    households and 40 anchor businesses currently off-grid."
    ```

    <Tip>
      Mention the technology, the geography, and who the project serves in your opening message. Nitrogen AI extracts geography, sector, and target population directly from this description — the more specific you are, the better the project plan and assessment recommendations will be.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Give your project a title">
    Rename the project from its header at any time — for example, "Rural Mini-Grid Kenya Phase 2." Nitrogen AI generates a default title from your description if you don't set one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Answer any follow-up questions">
    If your description leaves out something Nitrogen AI needs — like geography or budget range — it asks conversationally. You can answer several fields in a single message; the AI extracts and confirms each one.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  All project fields — title, geography, sector, goal, budget range, and timeline — can be edited at any time. Open the project and click the field you want to change, or mention it in chat.
</Note>

## Tips for a strong project description

<Tip>
  **Be specific about geography.** "Sub-Saharan Africa" is too broad. "Shire Highlands district, southern Malawi" lets the platform surface relevant policy context and comparable precedents.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Quantify your beneficiaries.** "Rural households" is a start; "approximately 3,500 rural households in 12 villages" is better. Scale and reach details improve the quality of assessments.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Name the technology.** Include the specific technology — solar home systems, biogas digesters, improved cookstoves, drip irrigation — rather than just the sector. This directly influences which assessments Nitrogen AI recommends.
</Tip>
