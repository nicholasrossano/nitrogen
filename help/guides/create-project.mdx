Creating a project is how you tell Nitrogen AI what you're working on. Instead of filling out a form, you have a short conversation — the platform asks you targeted questions and extracts the structured project profile it needs to power every assessment and document you generate. The whole intake takes a few minutes, and you can always refine your answers later.

## What you'll need before you start

You don't need any files ready before creating a project. The intake only requires information you already know about your work. That said, having these details at hand makes the conversation faster:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Project geography" icon="map-pin">
    The country or region where the project operates. Be as specific as you can — district or province level is better than just a country name.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Target population" icon="users">
    Who the project serves: households, smallholder farmers, health clinics, schools, and so on. Include an approximate number of beneficiaries if you have one.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Budget range" icon="coins">
    An approximate funding envelope. A range is fine — for example, $500K–$2M. You can update this after intake if figures change.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Project type" icon="solar-panel">
    The technology or intervention at the centre of the project, such as mini-grid electrification, clean cookstoves, solar irrigation, or climate adaptation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Create a new project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to your dashboard">
    Sign in to Nitrogen AI and go to your dashboard. You'll see all the projects you own or have been shared on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Project">
    Click **New Project** in the top-right corner of the dashboard. A new project record is created immediately and the intake chat opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Give your project a title">
    The platform prompts you to name your project. Enter a short, descriptive title — for example, "Rural Mini-Grid Kenya Phase 2" or "Solar Irrigation Burkina Faso". You can rename it at any time after creation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe your project">
    The chat opens with the prompt: *"Briefly describe your project."* Write a plain-language description of what the project is and what it's trying to achieve. The more specific you are here, the better the module recommendations will be later.

    <Tip>
      Mention the technology or intervention type, the geography, and who the project serves in your opening description. A sentence like "A 500 kW solar mini-grid in rural northern Ghana serving 2,000 households and 40 anchor businesses" gives the AI everything it needs to make strong recommendations.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Answer the intake questions">
    Nitrogen AI guides you through a conversational intake to collect the following details:

    | Field | What to provide |
    |---|---|
    | **Description** | What the project does and why |
    | **Geography** | Country or sub-national region of operation |
    | **Target population** | Communities or beneficiary groups the project serves |
    | **Project goal** | Primary objective or intended outcome |
    | **Budget range** | Approximate funding envelope |
    | **Timeline** | Expected duration or key milestones |
    | **Project type** | Technology, sector, or intervention category |

    You can answer multiple fields in a single message — the AI extracts and confirms each one. If your answer is ambiguous, it will ask a follow-up question.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the intake">
    Once all required fields are captured, the platform summarises the collected information and asks you to confirm. Review the summary for accuracy, then click **Confirm** (or type a confirmation in the chat).

    Confirming the intake moves your project to the **evidence** stage. The chat displays an evidence upload widget so you can continue straight away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your project title and all intake fields can be edited after creation. Open the project, click the title in the header to rename it, or return to the intake section of the chat to update individual fields.
</Note>

## Tips for a strong intake

<Tip>
  **Be specific about geography.** "Sub-Saharan Africa" is too broad. "Shire Highlands district, southern Malawi" lets the platform surface relevant policy, grid data, and comparable precedents.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Quantify your beneficiaries.** "Rural households" is good; "approximately 3,500 rural households in 12 villages" is better. Scale and reach details improve the quality of financial models and impact assessments.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Name the technology or intervention type.** Include the specific technology — solar home systems, biogas digesters, improved cookstoves, drip irrigation — rather than just the sector. This directly influences which modules Nitrogen AI recommends.
</Tip>

## What happens next

After confirming the intake, Nitrogen AI moves your project to the evidence stage. Upload your supporting documents — feasibility studies, technical reports, financial models — to ground every deliverable in your actual project data.
