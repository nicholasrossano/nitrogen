---
title: 'Export deliverables and share projects'
description: 'Download generated deliverables as DOCX or XLSX files, and invite teammates by assigning editor or viewer roles to collaborate on your project.'
---

Once your deliverables are generated, you can download them as formatted files ready for investors, donors, or internal review. You can also share your project with teammates on Nitrogen AI, giving each person the right level of access to view or collaborate on the work.

## Export a deliverable

Every generated deliverable has an **Export** button in the output widget. Click it and your browser downloads the file immediately — no additional steps required.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your project">
    Navigate to the project containing the deliverable you want to export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the deliverable in the chat">
    Scroll through the project chat to find the output widget for the module you want to export. Each widget shows the module name and a timestamp.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Export">
    Click the **Export** button on the widget. The file is generated from your project data and downloads immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Export formats by module

| Deliverable | Export format |
|---|---|
| Investment Memo | DOCX |
| Due Diligence Checklist | XLSX |
| LCOE Model | XLSX (inputs and calculated outputs) |
| Carbon Model | XLSX (inputs and calculated outputs) |
| Solar Estimate | XLSX (inputs and PVWatts results) |
| Stakeholder Assessment | DOCX |
| Landscape Mapping | DOCX |
| ESMP | DOCX |
| MEL Plan | DOCX |
| Template Fill | DOCX or XLSX (matches your original template format) |

<Note>
  Exports reflect the most recently generated version of each deliverable. If you regenerate a module, the next export will contain the updated content. Previous exports you've already downloaded are not affected.
</Note>

<Warning>
  For quantitative models (LCOE, Carbon, Solar), if the export returns a "model data unavailable" error, open the model widget in the chat and click **Recalculate** to refresh the data, then try exporting again.
</Warning>

## Share a project with teammates

You can share any project you own with other Nitrogen AI users. Sharing gives them access to the project in their own dashboard, with permissions determined by the role you assign.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open project settings">
    Open your project and click the **Share** button in the project header.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the teammate's email address">
    Type the email address of the person you want to invite. They must already have a Nitrogen AI account — sharing grants access immediately and does not send an invitation email to create a new account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a role">
    Select one of two roles:

    - **Editor** — can upload evidence, select modules, provide inputs, generate deliverables, and export. Best for active collaborators.
    - **Viewer** — read-only access. Can view the project and download exports but cannot make changes or run generation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Share">
    Click **Share** to confirm. The project appears immediately in the teammate's shared projects list on their dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  The person you share with must already have a Nitrogen AI account. If they don't have one yet, ask them to sign up before you share.
</Info>

### Role permissions at a glance

| Permission | Owner | Editor | Viewer |
|---|---|---|---|
| View project and deliverables | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Download exports | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Upload evidence | Yes | Yes | No |
| Select modules and provide inputs | Yes | Yes | No |
| Generate deliverables | Yes | Yes | No |
| Edit project title and icon | Yes | Yes | No |
| Share project with others | Yes | Yes | No |
| Change a teammate's role | Yes | No | No |
| Archive or delete the project | Yes | No | No |

## Update or remove access

You can change a collaborator's role or remove their access at any time from the Share panel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Share panel">
    Click **Share** in the project header to open the sharing settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the collaborator">
    The panel lists everyone who currently has access to the project, along with their roles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update or remove">
    - To change a role, select a new role from the dropdown next to the person's name.
    - To remove access, click the remove icon next to the person's name and confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Shared users can remove themselves from a project. If a collaborator no longer needs access, they can leave the project from their own dashboard without needing the owner to act.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Removing a user's access is immediate. They will lose access to the project and all of its deliverables as soon as you confirm the removal.
</Warning>
